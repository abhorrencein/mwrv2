Maitreyee's background spans macroeconomic analysis, urban and regional economics, behavioural economics, and transfer pricing advisory. She has worked across government, consulting, and international research organisations.

Her experience includes economic strategy work anchored by NITI Aayog for India's Vision 2047 growth hubs, third-party oversight of 18 state government scheme assessments in Gujarat, and spatial analysis of urbanisation patterns across 3,947 towns as a policy document for the Government of India. She has spent her career at the intersection of economics, strategy, and public policy.

Yashree is a research economist with experience in agricultural economics, quantitative analysis, survey design, and mixed-methods field research. She holds a PhD in Agricultural Economics from the University of Göttingen, followed by a postdoctoral research position there.

She has conducted primary field research in tribal villages across Gujarat and Maharashtra, managed large datasets using STATA and ArcGIS, and co-authored papers published in Economic & Political Weekly on groundwater governance and agrarian systems. Her earlier work includes positions at IWMI, the Asian Development Bank, and IIM Ahmedabad.
